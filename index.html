Why 2026 — the structural case
$94B FDI · 38 DFI pipelines · $1.4T infrastructure gap · no African-originating circuit
$94B
FDI committed to Africa 2023–25 · UNCTAD
Foreign direct investment into Africa sustained historic levels through a period when global FDI contracted by 18%. The continent is the outlier — not the exception.
38
Nations with active DFI pipelines · AfDB 2025
Development Finance Institutions have activated capital pipelines across 38 African jurisdictions. The infrastructure is in place. The convening infrastructure is not.
$1.4T
Infrastructure financing gap per year · African Union
The gap between Africa's infrastructure needs and committed financing is $1.4T annually. This is not a lack of capital — it is a lack of coordinated deployment mechanisms.
The convening gap
Every major financial conference covering Africa — AVCA, SuperReturn Africa, Africa CEO Forum — is organised from London, New York, or Geneva. The African Circuit is the first conceived, registered, and convened from within Africa itself. Mauritius, Nairobi, Cape Town, and Casablanca are not venues chosen for convenience. They are the four nodes through which institutional capital actually flows on this continent.

Sources: UNCTAD World Investment Report 2024 · AfDB Infrastructure Financing Report 2025 · African Union Commission · Casablanca Finance City Annual Review 2024
