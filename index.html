The case for 2026

The African capital conversation
has no home. Until now.

Every major financial conference covering Africa is organised from London, New York, or Geneva. The African Circuit is the first conceived, registered, and convened from within Africa itself.

01
No African-originating platform.
AVCA, SuperReturn Africa, Africa Financial Summit — all conceived and run from outside the continent. Principals who manage African capital have never had a circuit that starts from inside Africa, on African terms.
The African Circuit addresses this directly
02
Principals only. No exceptions.
Every delegate is a decision-maker. No consultants. No service providers unless invited as speakers. No press. 150–300 delegates per event, vetted by application — the room earns its value through who is excluded.
Delegate criteria enforced at application stage
03
Mauritius. 2026. Right now.
The founding-rate pricing window closes June 2026. The first event is September 2026. The platform is being built around the sponsors and delegates who arrive first — those who arrive second join someone else's circuit.
Founding-rate pricing · Closes 30 June 2026

Where African capital
is structured.

Mauritius is not a convenience — it is the structural foundation of the circuit. $800B+ in AUM administered, 26,000+ registered global business companies, and a treaty network that covers every major African market.

Privacy Policy · Summary

The African Circuit is operated by The Philanthropic Foundation, registered in Mauritius. We collect only what we need to run the circuit, communicate with you, and fulfil sponsorship and delegate agreements. We do not sell your data. We do not use it for advertising. You can request deletion at any time.

01
Who we are

The African Circuit is a pan-African financial conference circuit operated as an initiative of the Philanthropic Foundation, a company registered in Mauritius (Ebene Cyber City, Ebene, Republic of Mauritius). References to "we", "us", or "our" in this policy refer to the Philanthropic Foundation operating The African Circuit.

For questions about this policy or your personal data, contact us at partnerships@theafricancircuit.com.

02
What data we collect

We collect personal information in the following contexts:

Sponsorship and partnership enquiries — name, job title, institution, email address, and the sponsorship preferences you submit through our website or package builder.
Delegate registration — name, institution, role, contact details, and dietary or accessibility requirements for event logistics.
Website usage — standard server logs including IP address, browser type, pages visited, and time of visit. We do not use third-party analytics trackers.
Communications — email correspondence, meeting notes, and records of any agreements entered into with us.
Payment processing — billing name, institution, and transaction reference. Card details are processed exclusively by our payment provider and are never stored by us.
03
How we use your data

We use personal information only for the purposes for which it was collected:

To respond to sponsorship, partnership, and delegate enquiries.
To manage event registrations, issue delegate passes, and coordinate logistics.
To fulfil sponsorship agreements and deliver contracted benefits.
To send circuit-related communications — event updates, programme details, and post-event reports — to confirmed sponsors and registered delegates.
To maintain records required for our legal and financial obligations as a Mauritius-registered entity.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, automated decision-making, or any purpose unrelated to the operation of The African Circuit.

04
Legal basis for processing

We process personal data on the following legal grounds under applicable data protection law:

Contractual necessity — processing required to fulfil a sponsorship agreement or delegate registration.
Legitimate interests — responding to enquiries, circuit communications, and maintaining business records, where these interests are not overridden by your rights.
Legal obligation — record-keeping and compliance with Mauritius law and applicable international obligations.
Consent — where you have explicitly opted in to receive communications from us.
05
Who we share data with

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. We share it only in the following limited circumstances:

Event venues and logistics partners — name and dietary/accessibility requirements shared with five-star venue partners solely to deliver event services.
Service providers — email delivery, payment processing, and event platform providers who process data on our behalf under data processing agreements.
Co-sponsors — delegate lists shared with confirmed circuit sponsors in the form of post-event delegate reports, as described in sponsorship agreements. Reports contain name, institution, and role only.
Legal and regulatory authorities — where required by Mauritius law or a valid legal obligation.
06
International transfers

The African Circuit operates events across multiple African jurisdictions and globally online. Personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries where our event venues, sponsors, or service providers are located, including Kenya, South Africa, Morocco, and the UAE.

Where we transfer data outside Mauritius, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place — including contractual protections and, where applicable, adequacy determinations — to protect your data in accordance with Mauritius data protection law.

07
How long we keep your data
Sponsorship and delegate records — retained for 7 years from the date of the last event attended or agreement executed, in line with our legal and financial record-keeping obligations.
Enquiries that did not proceed — retained for 12 months from initial contact, then deleted unless you have requested otherwise.
Marketing and circuit communications — retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
Website logs — retained for 90 days, then automatically deleted.
08
Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Correction — to request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected.
Deletion — to request that your data be deleted, where we are not required to retain it by law.
Restriction — to request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
Objection — to object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
Portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
Withdrawal of consent — to withdraw any consent you have given at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at partnerships@theafricancircuit.com. We will respond within 30 days.

09
Cookies and tracking

This website does not use advertising cookies, third-party analytics, or tracking pixels. We use only essential session functionality required for the website to operate. No consent banner is required as no non-essential cookies are set.

10
Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These include encrypted data transmission (TLS), access controls, and regular security reviews. In the event of a data breach that affects your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and any relevant regulatory authority in accordance with applicable law.

11
Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. The current version is always available at this page. Material changes will be communicated to confirmed sponsors and registered delegates by email.

The Investment Thesis · 2026

Africa's institutional capital moment is now.

A structural argument for why 2026 is the inflection year — and why the principals who convene now will define the terms for the decade that follows.

01 · The structural shift

Three forces converging simultaneously

$94B
FDI committed to Africa 2023–25 · UNCTAD
Foreign direct investment into Africa has sustained historic levels through a period when global FDI contracted by 18%. The continent is the outlier — not the exception.
38
African nations with active DFI pipelines · AfDB 2025
Development Finance Institutions have activated capital pipelines across 38 African jurisdictions. The infrastructure is in place. The convening infrastructure is not.
$1.4T
Infrastructure financing gap per year · African Union
The gap between Africa's infrastructure needs and committed financing is $1.4T annually. This is not a lack of capital — it is a lack of coordinated deployment mechanisms.
02 · The convening gap

Capital is there. Coordination is not.

The defining African finance events of the past decade — AVCA, AfDB Annual Meetings, Africa CEO Forum — are organised outside the continent, by institutions whose primary constituency is outside the continent.

This creates a structural information asymmetry: the principals who actually move capital into Africa are convening on terms set by those who observe it. The African Circuit is the first institution to correct this — a circuit that originates from African soil, governed by an African foundation, convening where the capital is deployed.

Mauritius, Nairobi, Cape Town, and Casablanca are not conference venues chosen for convenience. They are the four nodes through which institutional capital actually flows on this continent.

Mauritius
Africa's treaty and capital gateway
46 double taxation treaties. The jurisdiction through which $8B+ in African private equity is structured annually.
Nairobi
East Africa's fintech and VC hub
Home to M-Pesa, the IFC's East Africa office, and the fastest-growing startup ecosystem on the continent.
Cape Town
Southern Africa's ESG and infrastructure capital
JSE-listed pension mandates, PIC, and the continent's deepest institutional capital base. ESG reporting infrastructure that rivals European standards.
Casablanca
North Africa's sovereign and francophone gateway
Casablanca Finance City hosts 200+ multinational financial institutions. The entry point to sovereign wealth, Islamic finance, and francophone Africa's $600B+ economy.
03 · The founding argument
Every institution that defines a market is founded at the moment the market becomes a market.

Davos was founded in 1971, the year Bretton Woods collapsed and a new global financial architecture was being written. SuperReturn was founded in 1998, the year European private equity institutionalised. AVCA's founding conference coincided with the first wave of pan-African fund launches.

The African institutional capital moment is not coming. It is here. The principals who convene in Mauritius in September 2026 will not be attending the African Circuit. They will be founding it — and the terms they set, the relationships they build, and the deals they initiate will define the market structure for the decade that follows.

Governance & Independence

Built for principals. Answerable to no one else.

The African Circuit is editorially independent, institutionally governed, and operated under a strict code of conduct. This page exists for compliance teams, DFI procurement officers, and delegates who need to verify before they commit.

01 · Structure

Institutional foundation

The African Circuit is an initiative of The Philanthropic Foundation, a registered entity in Mauritius operating under the Non-Profit Organisations Act 2012. The Foundation's mandate covers financial inclusion, institutional capacity-building, and the development of African capital markets.

The circuit is governed by an independent programme committee responsible for speaker selection, delegate vetting, and editorial independence. Sponsors have no editorial input — speaker slots are never sold, and panels are never commercially influenced.

The Foundation is affiliated with Transition Africa Partners (TAP Fund I), a pan-African private equity vehicle focused on transition finance and infrastructure.

The Philanthropic Foundation → Transition Africa Partners · TAP Fund I →
02 · Conduct

Rules of the room

Chatham House Rule — all sessions
Information shared in sessions may be used but the identity and affiliation of the speaker may not be disclosed. No recording, no attribution without explicit consent.
No press access — ever
Journalists, media organisations, and public affairs professionals are not eligible for delegate positions. There are no press credentials. What is said in the room stays in the room.
Editorial independence
Sponsors do not select, influence, or approve speakers or panel topics. All programming decisions rest with the independent programme committee. Sponsorship funds operations — it does not purchase influence.
Anti-bribery and sanctions compliance
All delegates and sponsors are screened against OFAC, UN, EU, and UK sanctions lists. The circuit does not accept participation from sanctioned individuals or entities. KYC documentation is required for corporate sponsorships above $25,000.
Data protection — Mauritius DPA 2017
Delegate data is held under the Mauritius Data Protection Act 2017, consistent with GDPR principles. No delegate information is shared with sponsors. Attendee lists are never sold or licensed.
